Plain Talk

Chiefs are skeptical of "free."
That's the right instinct. Here's the honest answer.

You've seen vendors offer free things that disappear, convert to paid, or come with strings. We're not going to just say "no strings" and expect you to take our word for it. Here's exactly how this works.

The cost reality

The infrastructure cost is genuinely low

Running email, candidate records, tagging, a recruiting web page, and a job board listing for a law enforcement agency costs a few dollars a month — maybe less. We built this system already. Adding member agencies to the free tier doesn't cost us much to operate. We're not absorbing a significant loss. We're offering something that costs us very little but is genuinely useful to your members.

Where this came from

Built from real campaigns, not a pitch deck

Before there was software, we ran law enforcement recruitment campaigns for some of the largest agencies in the country. We built this platform to support a statewide hiring initiative for the State of Delaware. The software didn't come first — the real work did. We learned what agencies actually needed by doing it, then built the tools that solved it.

Our business model

About 15% of free agencies upgrade. That's enough.

We expect about 15% of agencies on the free plan will eventually upgrade to Pro — for SMS, events, automation, or analytics. That pays for everything. The other 85% can use the free plan forever. If a member agency never upgrades, we still want them using this. Safer communities are the point. Revenue comes when we've earned it.

Member agencies can use the free version forever. Upgrade when it makes sense — or don't.

No sales calls every 30 days. No trial period that flips to paid. No feature that disappears next quarter. The free plan stays free. If Pro ever becomes worth it to an agency, they'll know.

How rollout works

Getting a statewide job board live for your members is simpler than it sounds. Here is the typical path from first conversation to launch.

1

Intro Briefing

We walk your team through the platform, show examples of active job boards in other states, and answer questions. Thirty minutes. No commitment required.

2

Job Board Setup

We configure a branded law enforcement job board for your state. No technical work required from your side — we handle the setup and send you a link to review before it goes live.

3

Member Agency Onboarding

Member agencies are invited to claim their free account, set up their profile, and post their first open position. Each agency is self-serve from day one — no IT support required.

4

Launch

The job board goes live. Candidates can start searching and applying immediately. Your association announces it to members however you normally communicate — email, newsletter, conference.

5

Ongoing Adoption

We work with you to onboard additional agencies over time. No pressure, no deadlines. As more agencies participate, the job board becomes more valuable for candidates and more useful for your association.

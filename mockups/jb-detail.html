Position Overview

As an entry-level Police Officer with the Austin Police Department, you will be at the forefront of protecting one of the fastest-growing cities in the United States. Assigned to the Patrol Division, officers respond to a wide variety of calls for service — from traffic incidents and property crimes to medical assists and domestic disturbances — while proactively engaging with the communities they serve. You will patrol designated geographic areas by vehicle and on foot, building relationships with residents and businesses that are essential to Austin PD's community-oriented policing philosophy.

Day-to-day duties include conducting preliminary criminal investigations, gathering and preserving evidence, interviewing witnesses and complainants, and preparing thorough, accurate reports. Officers are expected to exercise sound judgment and de-escalation skills in dynamic, rapidly evolving situations. You will work closely with detectives, crisis intervention teams, and social service partners to ensure the best possible outcomes for all parties involved.

Austin PD embraces a data-driven, problem-solving approach to public safety. Officers participate in community outreach events, neighborhood meetings, and collaborative programs with local schools and organizations. You will have the opportunity to build a career in one of the nation's most recognized and progressive police departments, with pathways to specialized units including investigations, K-9, mounted patrol, SWAT, and community liaison roles.

All newly hired officers attend the Austin Police Training Academy — a rigorous 22-week paid program covering Texas law, defensive tactics, firearms proficiency, emergency vehicle operations, crisis intervention, and community policing strategies. Upon graduation, recruits complete a structured field training program under the supervision of certified Field Training Officers before assuming independent patrol duties.

Minimum Requirements
  • Must be at least 21 years of age at time of appointment
  • U.S. Citizen or authorized to work in the United States
  • High school diploma or GED required; 45+ college credit hours preferred
  • Valid Texas driver's license with an acceptable driving record
  • No felony convictions; no Class A misdemeanor convictions within the past 10 years
  • No Class B misdemeanor convictions within the past 5 years
  • Must pass a physical fitness assessment, written examination, and oral review board
  • Must pass a comprehensive background investigation, polygraph exam, and psychological evaluation
  • Vision correctable to 20/20 in each eye; normal color and depth perception required
  • Must be able to obtain and maintain TCOLE peace officer license (Department will sponsor)
Preferred Qualifications
  • U.S. military experience with honorable discharge — demonstrates discipline, teamwork, and service commitment
  • Bilingual in Spanish and English — strongly preferred given Austin's diverse community demographics
  • Prior law enforcement or security experience at any level
  • Associate's or Bachelor's degree in Criminal Justice, Public Safety Administration, or a related field
  • Community involvement or volunteer service in public safety or youth programs
  • Experience with crisis intervention, mental health first aid, or social services
Compensation & Benefits
  • Starting salary $62,400/yr — annual step increases reaching $78,000+ within 7 years of service
  • Comprehensive health, dental, and vision insurance for employee and eligible dependents, with multiple plan tiers
  • Texas Municipal Retirement System (TMRS) defined-benefit pension — City contributes 2:1 match on employee contributions
  • Paid Academy Training — 22-week training program at full starting salary; no out-of-pocket costs for uniforms or equipment
  • 15 days vacation + 15 days sick leave per year, accruing immediately; additional longevity pay after 5 years
  • Tuition reimbursement program — up to $5,250/yr for approved degree programs in law enforcement or public administration
  • Hazard pay, shift differential, overtime opportunities, and bilingual pay supplement available
About Austin Police Department
APD

The Austin Police Department is one of the largest and most respected law enforcement agencies in Texas, with approximately 850 sworn officers serving a city of over 1 million residents. Founded in 1839, APD is nationally accredited by the Commission on Accreditation for Law Enforcement Agencies (CALEA) and has been recognized for its commitment to community-oriented policing, crisis intervention training, and innovative public safety partnerships.

APD operates 24 hours a day, 365 days a year across patrol, investigations, special operations, and administrative divisions. The Department is dedicated to building trust through transparency, community engagement, and continuous professional development. Austin PD is an equal opportunity employer actively recruiting qualified candidates who reflect the diverse communities they serve.

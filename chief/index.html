Self-serve doesn't work. Guided does.

When agencies self-serve a recruiting platform, they log in once, see a blank tool, and never come back. We've seen it. The platform isn't the problem — the lack of setup is. We built this platform to support a statewide hiring initiative for the State of Delaware. The software didn't come first — the real work did. We learned what agencies actually needed by doing it, then built the tools that solved it.

How the guided launch works
Four calls. Four weeks.
Fully operational before the trial ends.

Every member agency that enrolls is assigned a dedicated launch specialist. The four-call process takes agencies from zero to a live recruiting operation in about 30 days — before they've spent a dollar.

1

Discovery — Positions, Stages & Communication Needs

We learn your agency's open positions, how candidates move through your hiring process, and what communication you're sending today (if any). This becomes the blueprint for everything we build in the platform.

2

Automated Communication Review & Approval

We build your automated email sequences — application confirmations, follow-ups, stage-based messages — and walk you through them. You review, request changes, and approve before anything goes live. Your voice, your timing, your process.

3

Integration into Current Digital Platforms

We connect All-Star Recruiter to your existing digital presence — website, social channels, Guardian Alliance account, and any job boards you're already using. Candidates coming from any source land in the same pipeline.

4

Training & Best Practices

A live walkthrough with your recruiter or HR contact. We cover day-to-day workflows, how to read the pipeline, what to do when a candidate goes quiet, and how to get the most out of the platform going forward. Your team leaves the call running it — not hoping they figure it out.

Agencies must commit to all four calls. We do the heavy lifting — agencies bring their recruiter or HR contact to each session. If an agency isn't willing to show up, the trial isn't the right next step yet. That's by design.
